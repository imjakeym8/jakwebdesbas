What I learned:

  • Before and After pseudo elements are OUTSIDE the content of an element. This means its also outside padding, border, and even margin.
  • If you wish to put a solid shape as a background for content then decided to add text after, Before and After pseudo elements will come in handy to when adding text
  • Before means before the content and After means after; its kinda obvious, but I found out that in its default setting, the before pseudo is normally placed above the content and after pseudo below the content. This can be fixed with "display:;" property.
  • BONUS: CSS Grid works best with as many grids as possible; I learned the hard way that styling with one to two rows/columns using Grid is very time consuming. It would've been better to use "display: inline block;" with float properties or "display: flex;".
